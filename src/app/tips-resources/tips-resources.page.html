Street Waste Recycling

Learn how to safely collect and handle waste from the streets with care before delivering it to the correct disposal or recycling points.

1. Wear Protective Gear

Always use gloves, closed shoes, and a face mask when handling waste from the street to avoid direct contact with harmful substances.

Source: US EPA – How Do I Recycle Common Recyclables

2. Separate Recyclables On the Spot

Use different bags or containers for plastics, metals, glass, and organic waste while collecting. This saves time during sorting later.

Source: Seattle Public Utilities – Recycle Right

3. Avoid Sharp or Dangerous Objects

Never pick up broken glass, needles, or sharp metals with bare hands. Use tongs or a broom and dustpan for safety.

Source: City of Saint Paul – What Goes in My Recycling Cart?

4. Rinse Recyclables

If possible, rinse bottles and containers with clean water to reduce smell and contamination before storing them.

Source: US EPA – How Do I Recycle Common Recyclables

5. Use Clearly Marked Containers

Label containers or bags according to material types so waste handlers at the site know how to handle them properly.

Source: Seattle Public Utilities – Recycle Right

6. Transport with Care

Secure waste bags properly before transporting them. Avoid spillage by using covered bins or sacks that won’t tear.

Source: US EPA – How Do I Recycle Common Recyclables

7. Drop at Approved Sites

Only take collected waste to approved dumping or recycling centers. Never leave it in unregulated places or open landfills.

Source: City of Saint Paul – What Goes in My Recycling Cart?
